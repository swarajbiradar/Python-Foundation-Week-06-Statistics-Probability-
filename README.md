# 📊 Statistics for Data Science – 6 Day Roadmap

## 🚀 Overview
This repository contains a structured 6-day learning roadmap covering essential statistical concepts required for data analysis and machine learning. The focus is on understanding data behavior, probability, distributions, and statistical inference through hands-on practice.

---

## 📅 Day 1: Mean, Median, Variance, Standard Deviation

### 🔹 Concepts
- Mean: Average of data points
- Median: Middle value in sorted data
- Variance: Measure of data spread
- Standard Deviation: Square root of variance (dispersion)

### 🔹 Practice
- Compute mean and median for datasets
- Calculate variance and standard deviation step-by-step
- Compare datasets with different spreads

### 🔹 Example
- Analyze exam scores of 10 students using all measures

### 📌 Submission
Notebook with at least 5 tasks using descriptive statistics

---

## 📅 Day 2: Probability Basics

### 🔹 Concepts
- Probability: Likelihood of an event (0 to 1)
- Types of events: Independent, dependent
- Rules:
  - Addition rule
  - Multiplication rule

### 🔹 Practice
- Solve probability problems (dice, coins)
- Work with multiple events
- Apply probability rules

### 🔹 Example
- Probability of rolling a 4 on a die
- Probability of getting two heads consecutively

### 📌 Submission
Notebook with at least 5 probability problems

---

## 📅 Day 3: Probability Distributions

### 🔹 Concepts
- Normal Distribution: Symmetric bell-shaped curve
- Binomial Distribution: Repeated success/failure trials
- Poisson Distribution: Rare events in fixed intervals

### 🔹 Practice
- Plot normal distribution curves
- Simulate binomial experiments
- Model Poisson events

### 🔹 Examples
- Exam scores (Normal)
- Coin toss outcomes (Binomial)
- Calls per hour (Poisson)

### 📌 Submission
Notebook with at least 5 distribution-based tasks

---

## 📅 Day 4: Sampling & Central Limit Theorem

### 🔹 Concepts
- Sampling: Selecting subset from population
- Central Limit Theorem (CLT):
  - Sample means approximate normal distribution
  - Works regardless of population distribution (large samples)

### 🔹 Practice
- Draw random samples
- Compute sample means
- Visualize distribution of sample means

### 🔹 Example
- Average height from random groups

### 📌 Submission
Notebook with at least 5 sampling and CLT tasks

---

## 📅 Day 5: Hypothesis Testing

### 🔹 Concepts
- Hypothesis Testing:
  - Null Hypothesis (H₀)
  - Alternative Hypothesis (H₁)
- t-test: Compare means
- Chi-square test: Compare categorical data

### 🔹 Practice
- Perform t-test on two datasets
- Apply chi-square test
- Interpret p-values and significance

### 🔹 Examples
- Compare average marks of two classes
- Analyze preference (tea vs coffee)

Notebook with at least 5 hypothesis testing tasks

### 🔹 Concepts
- Applying all statistical techniques in one workflow

### 🔹 Practice
- Compute descriptive statistics
- Apply probability concepts
- Identify distributions
- Perform hypothesis testing
- Document insights clearly

### 🔹 Example
